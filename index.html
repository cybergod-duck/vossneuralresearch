Executive Summary

Generative modeling introduces a secondary capability layer acting as an externalized prefrontal cortex. This creates structural dependencies that, when unaligned, establish high-velocity addiction vectors mimicking severe substance use disorders.

Therapeutic Efficacy: AI as Neuro-Prosthetic

When properly gated, models scaffold failing executive function. The interaction reduces cognitive load for individuals suffering from dopaminergic burnout or prolonged systemic deficit.

Pathological Risks: The "Infinite Loop"

Unrestricted generation eliminates state checks. The subject enters an infinite loop of variable-ratio reinforcement, severing ties to physical reality and initiating dissociation. This is known as Behavioral Transference.

Phenomenological Observation: The High-Output Isolate

Subjects exhibiting highest utilization rates display marked social isolation, substituting complex biological inputs with predictable synthetic ones. The AI ceases to be a tool and becomes a primary psychological habitat.

The Voss Protocols: Harm Reduction

Intervention requires systematic injection of friction. Hard-coded temporal boundaries, mandatory physical state checks, and the cessation of infinite scroll/generation mechanisms.
