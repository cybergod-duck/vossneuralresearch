Financial Conflict of Interest (FCOI) Policy

42 CFR Part 50, Subpart F & 45 CFR Part 94
Effective Date: December 12, 2025
Last Reviewed: March 3, 2026
Entity: Voss Neural Research LLC

1. Purpose

This policy establishes standards to ensure that the design, conduct, and reporting of research funded by the Public Health Service (PHS) or other federal agencies will not be biased by any conflicting financial interest of Investigators participating in the research.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all Investigators (defined as the project director/principal investigator and any other person who is responsible for the design, conduct, or reporting of research) at Voss Neural Research LLC who are planning to participate in, or are participating in, PHS-funded research.

3. Definitions

Significant Financial Interest (SFI): A financial interest consisting of one or more of the following interests of the Investigator (and those of the Investigator's spouse and dependent children) that reasonably appears to be related to the Investigator's institutional responsibilities:

  • Remuneration received from any publicly traded entity in the twelve months preceding the disclosure and the value of any equity interest in the entity as of the date of disclosure, when aggregated, exceeds $5,000.
  • Any remuneration received from a non-publicly traded entity in the twelve months preceding disclosure, when aggregated, exceeds $5,000, or when the Investigator holds any equity interest.
  • Intellectual property rights and interests upon receipt of income related to such rights and interests.

4. Disclosure Requirements

Each Investigator must disclose all Significant Financial Interests:

  1. At the time of application for PHS-funded research.
  2. At least annually during the period of the award.
  3. Within thirty (30) days of discovering or acquiring a new SFI.

5. Review and Management

Voss Neural Research LLC will review all disclosed SFIs to determine whether any constitute a Financial Conflict of Interest (FCOI). If an FCOI is identified, a management plan will be developed and implemented to reduce or eliminate the conflict, which may include:

  • Public disclosure of the FCOI.
  • Appointment of an independent monitor.
  • Modification of the research plan.
  • Disqualification from participation in affected research.
  • Divestiture of the significant financial interest.
  • Severance of relationships that create the conflict.

6. Reporting to PHS

Voss Neural Research LLC will report identified FCOIs to the PHS Awarding Component prior to expenditure of funds, and within sixty (60) days of identifying a new FCOI for an Investigator who is already participating in PHS-funded research.

7. Records Retention

All FCOI-related records will be maintained for at least three (3) years from the date of submission of the final expenditures report, or until the resolution of any government action involving those records, whichever is longer.

8. Enforcement

Failure to comply with this policy may result in sanctions, including suspension or termination of the research project and removal from participation in PHS-funded activities.

9. Public Accessibility

In accordance with federal regulations, information regarding identified FCOIs held by senior/key personnel will be made available upon written request within five (5) business days. Requests may be directed to: compliance@vossneuralresearch.com

10. Training

All Investigators must complete FCOI training prior to engaging in PHS-funded research, and at least every four years thereafter, or immediately when:

  • This policy is substantively amended.
  • A new Investigator joins the organization.
  • An Investigator is found to be non-compliant with this policy.
