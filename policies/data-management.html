Data Management & Sharing Plan

NIH Data Management and Sharing (DMS) Policy Compliant
Effective Date: December 12, 2025
Last Reviewed: March 3, 2026
Entity: Voss Neural Research LLC

1. Data Types and Scope

Voss Neural Research generates the following categories of scientific data in the course of its research activities:

Data Type Format Description
Behavioral Telemetry JSON, CSV Timestamped interaction velocity, session duration, engagement pattern metrics from AI-human interaction studies.
Protocol Outcome Data CSV, PDF Somatic Anchoring (SA-01) intervention efficacy measurements, executive function recovery indices.
AI Model Output Logs JSON, TXT Multi-model synthesis engine response data, convergent signal analysis records.
Variable Reward Architecture Profiles JSON, PDF Forensic analyses of commercial AI platform engagement mechanics.

2. Standards and Formats

All scientific data will be maintained in machine-readable, non-proprietary formats (CSV, JSON, plain text) to maximize interoperability and long-term accessibility. Metadata will follow the Dublin Core Metadata Element Set and will include provenance, methodology, and temporal information.

3. Data Storage and Preservation

Research data is stored using encrypted cloud storage with automated versioning and backup. Data will be preserved for a minimum of:

  • SBIR/STTR data: Protected for 20 years under the SBIR/STTR data rights clause, during which VNR retains exclusive rights to use, modify, and distribute the data.
  • Published data: Maintained indefinitely in accessible repositories.
  • Clinical/behavioral data: Retained for a minimum of 10 years following completion of the study, or as required by the funding agency.

4. Data Sharing and Access

Voss Neural Research is committed to sharing research data to the greatest extent permissible, consistent with privacy protections, intellectual property rights, and SBIR/STTR data protection provisions. Data sharing will be accomplished through:

  • Publication of summarized datasets alongside peer-reviewed manuscripts.
  • Deposit of de-identified datasets in appropriate public repositories upon expiration of SBIR/STTR data protection periods.
  • Direct data sharing agreements with qualified researchers upon written request.

5. Privacy and Human Subjects Protection

All research involving human subjects data, including N=1 longitudinal studies, will comply with applicable regulations including:

  • The Common Rule (45 CFR Part 46).
  • HIPAA Privacy Rule where applicable.
  • De-identification standards per the Safe Harbor method.

Behavioral telemetry data that could identify research subjects will be de-identified prior to any sharing or publication.

6. Oversight and Responsibility

The Principal Investigator (Tim Chappell, Founder) is responsible for the implementation and compliance of this Data Management and Sharing Plan. Inquiries may be directed to: data@vossneuralresearch.com
